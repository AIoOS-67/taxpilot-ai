# Tax Credits Guide — Tax Year 2025

Source: IRS Publications 596, 972, 970, 5695

Credits directly reduce tax owed (dollar-for-dollar), unlike deductions which reduce taxable income.

## Earned Income Tax Credit (EITC)

**Refundable** — can result in a refund even if no tax owed.

| Children | Max Credit | Max AGI (Single) | Max AGI (MFJ) |
|---|---|---|---|
| 0 | $649 | $19,104 | $26,214 |
| 1 | $4,328 | $50,434 | $57,554 |
| 2 | $7,152 | $57,310 | $64,430 |
| 3+ | $8,046 | $61,555 | $68,675 |

Requirements:
- Must have earned income
- Investment income must be ≤ $11,950
- Must be US citizen/resident alien for full year
- Cannot file Form 2555 (foreign earned income)
- IRS Reference: Publication 596

## Child Tax Credit (CTC)

- **$2,000** per qualifying child under age 17
- Refundable portion (ACTC): up to **$1,700**
- Phase-out begins at $200,000 (single) / $400,000 (MFJ)
- Phase-out rate: $50 per $1,000 over threshold
- IRS Reference: Publication 972

## Child and Dependent Care Credit

- 20-35% of qualifying expenses (percentage decreases with income)
- Max expenses: **$3,000** (one dependent) / **$6,000** (two+)
- Max credit: $1,050 (one) / $2,100 (two+)
- Both spouses must work (or be students/disabled)
- IRS Reference: Publication 503

## American Opportunity Tax Credit (AOTC)

- Up to **$2,500** per eligible student
- 100% of first $2,000 + 25% of next $2,000 in tuition/fees
- 40% refundable (up to $1,000)
- First 4 years of college only
- Phase-out: $80,000-$90,000 (single), $160,000-$180,000 (MFJ)
- IRS Reference: Publication 970

## Lifetime Learning Credit (LLC)

- Up to **$2,000** per return (not per student)
- 20% of first $10,000 in tuition/fees
- No limit on years claimed
- Phase-out: $80,000-$90,000 (single), $160,000-$180,000 (MFJ)
- Cannot claim both AOTC and LLC for same student
- IRS Reference: Publication 970

## Residential Clean Energy Credit

- **30%** of qualifying costs (no annual maximum)
- Solar panels, solar water heaters, battery storage (3 kWh+)
- Wind turbines, geothermal heat pumps, fuel cells
- Available through 2032 (steps down after)
- IRS Reference: Form 5695, Part I

## Energy Efficient Home Improvement Credit

- **30%** of costs, up to **$3,200/year**
- Sub-limits: $1,200 for most items, $2,000 for heat pumps
- Insulation, windows, doors, heat pumps, water heaters
- IRS Reference: Form 5695, Part II

## Saver's Credit (Retirement Savings Contributions Credit)

- 10-50% of retirement contributions, up to $2,000 per person
- AGI limits (2025): $39,500 (single), $59,250 (HoH), $79,000 (MFJ)
- Must be age 18+, not a student, not claimed as dependent
- IRS Reference: Form 8880

## Premium Tax Credit (ACA)

- For marketplace health insurance purchased through Healthcare.gov
- Based on income relative to federal poverty level
- Claim on Form 8962
- If advance credit was received, must reconcile on return
