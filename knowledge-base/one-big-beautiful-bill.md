# One Big Beautiful Bill Act — 2025 Tax Law Changes

Source: H.R. 1 (119th Congress), IRS guidance, CBO analysis

## Overview

The "One Big Beautiful Bill Act" is major tax legislation passed in 2025, extending and modifying provisions of the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (TCJA) while adding new provisions.

## Key Provisions

### No Tax on Tips
- Qualifying tipped workers can deduct cash tips from taxable income
- Cap: up to $25,000 in tip income annually
- Eligibility: workers in occupations that traditionally receive tips
- Does not affect FICA taxes on tips
- Effective: tax years beginning after December 31, 2024

### No Tax on Overtime (Proposed)
- Overtime pay (hours >40/week) would be tax-exempt
- Income cap for eligibility being debated
- Would only apply to W-2 employees
- Status: under negotiation as of early 2026

### No Tax on Social Security (Proposed)
- Would eliminate federal tax on Social Security benefits
- Currently: up to 85% of SS can be taxable
- Cost estimate: $1.5T over 10 years
- Status: being negotiated

### SALT Deduction Changes
- Current cap: $10,000 ($5,000 MFS)
- Proposed increase: up to $40,000 for filers with AGI under $500,000
- This is a significant change for taxpayers in high-tax states (CA, NY, NJ, CT)
- Phase-out proposed for higher incomes

### Standard Deduction
- Maintained at increased 2025 levels
- Single: $15,000 / MFJ: $30,000 / HoH: $22,500
- Potential further increases under discussion

### Child Tax Credit
- Current: $2,000 per qualifying child
- Proposed increase: up to $2,500-$3,000 per child
- Expanded refundable portion
- Phase-out thresholds may increase

### Business Provisions
- Section 199A (QBI) deduction: extended and potentially expanded
- Bonus depreciation: returning to 100% (was phasing down)
- R&D expensing: restored (was being amortized over 5 years)

### Estate Tax
- Exemption maintained at ~$13.99M per person
- Would have dropped to ~$7M without extension

## Impact on Tax Filing

1. **Check tip income status** — If you receive tips, the deduction may apply
2. **Recalculate SALT** — Higher cap means more potential deduction for high-tax states
3. **CTC increase** — Families may get larger credit
4. **Wait for IRS guidance** — Some provisions may have delayed effective dates

## Timeline

- Bill introduced: 2025
- Key provisions effective: Tax year 2025 and beyond
- Some provisions retroactive to January 1, 2025
- IRS forms and publications being updated throughout 2026
- TaxPilot AI will apply the latest confirmed provisions to your return

Tax law is subject to change. Some provisions of the One Big Beautiful Bill Act were still being finalized, amended, or interpreted by the IRS as of early 2026. TaxPilot AI uses the most current confirmed provisions. Items with uncertainty are flagged for professional review.
